Diagnosing Pulmonary Embolism on CT

Arun Nachiappan MD

Goal

Develop a systematic approach to accurately interpret CT Pulmonary Angiogram

Objectives

  • Assess quality of CT exam by determining which pulmonary artery level can be clearly seen to, while identifying limitations
  • Compare and contrast features of acute and chronic pulmonary embolism, and distinguish those from artifact
  • Discuss ancillary findings, and evaluate for right ventricular strain

Overview

  • Pulmonary embolism (PE)
  • Pulmonary artery (PA) anatomy
  • CT pulmonary angiogram (CTPA) technique

PE

  • Embolization of venous thrombus to a PA
  • Usually from proximal lower extremity (LE) deep venous thrombus (DVT)

PE epidemiology

  • Incidence in the US is 112 cases/100,000/year
  • Venous thromboembolism (VTE) is 3rd most common acute cardiovascular disease in the world

PE adverse outcomes

  • Death
    • Untreated: up to 30%
    • Treated with anticoagulation: 2%-11%
  • Recurrent PE and DVT while on anticoagulant therapy
    • 6% in first 3 months
  • Chronic thromboembolic pulmonary hypertension
    • 1%-4% of acute PE survivors after 3 months to 2 years

PA names/levels simplified

  • Central
    • Main PA (MPA)
    • Right PA (RPA) and Left PA (LPA)
    • Right Interlobar PA and Left Interlobar PA
    • Lobar level
  • Peripheral
    • Segmental level
    • Subsegmental level
